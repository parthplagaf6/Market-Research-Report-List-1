<p><p>The percutaneous indwelling central catheter market features primarily two types: Conventional PICC and Power-Injectable PICC. Conventional PICCs are used for long-term venous access, suitable for administering medications and fluids without high-pressure injections. In contrast, Power-Injectable PICCs are designed to withstand higher pressure, allowing for quicker infusion of contrast media in imaging procedures. The choice between these types depends on the treatment requirements and the specific clinical context, influencing their adoption in healthcare settings.</p></p>
