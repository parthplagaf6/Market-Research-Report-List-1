<p><p>The RF and Microwave Ceramic Capacitors market is primarily segmented into Class 1 and Class 2 capacitors. Class 1 capacitors, characterized by their stable capacitance and low loss, are ideal for high-frequency applications where precision is critical. Class 2 capacitors, on the other hand, offer higher capacitance values but with more variability in temperature and voltage characteristics, making them suitable for applications requiring bulk capacitance at lower cost. Both types cater to diverse applications in telecommunications, automotive, and consumer electronics.</p></p>
