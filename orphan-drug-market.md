<p><p>The orphan drug market is categorized into two main types: biologics and non-biologics. Biologics are derived from living organisms and include products like monoclonal antibodies, vaccines, and gene therapies, often targeting rare diseases at a molecular level. Non-biologics consist of small molecules, synthetic drugs, and traditional pharmaceuticals used to treat rare conditions. Both types are developed to address unmet medical needs for individuals with rare diseases, often benefiting from incentives like extended market exclusivity and accelerated approval processes.</p></p>
