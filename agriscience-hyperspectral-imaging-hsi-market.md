<p><p>Agriscience hyperspectral imaging (HSI) involves capturing images across multiple wavelengths to analyze agricultural products and processes. The market is segmented into three main types: Visible Light and Near Infrared (VNIR), which focuses on visible light and the adjacent near-infrared spectrum, offering insights into crop health and stress; Short Wave Infrared (SWIR) targets longer wavelengths, aiding in moisture detection and material characterization. Together, these technologies enhance precision agriculture by providing detailed data for better decision-making and improved yields.</p></p>
